  - Final collision = False
  - Final state = (5.803932722590211, 1.9677625895405662, 2.9609685252017415)

  - Final executed cost = 77.404
  - Final collision = False
  - Final state = (5.6527050146556945, 2.1127455026262623, -1.2412552997105557)

Preliminary observation:
- `horizon = 25` gave the best balance among the three tested values.
- `horizon = 15` was too short and led to a much longer executed trajectory and a much higher final cost.
- `horizon = 35` remained feasible and collision-free, but did not outperform `horizon = 25`.
- In the current baseline, a too-short horizon hurts long-term planning, while a too-long horizon may make optimization harder under a fixed sample budget.

### What I understand now
- MPPI does not directly execute the whole updated sequence.
- It executes only the first control, then shifts the sequence and replans from the new real state.
- The shifted sequence is not the new sampled result itself; it becomes the next nominal sequence for the next round of sampling.
